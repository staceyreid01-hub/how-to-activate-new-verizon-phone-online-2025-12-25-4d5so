To activate your Verizon phone, you'll typically need: your phone's IMEI number (found in the phone's settings or on the original packaging), your Verizon account number or phone number, a valid form of identification, and your billing ZIP code. If you're transferring from another carrier, you may also need information about your previous carrier. Our step-by-step guide will walk you through exactly where to find each piece of information and how to use it during activation.

If you encounter any errors during the activation process, don't worry. Our comprehensive troubleshooting guide covers the most common issues and their solutions. Common problems include incorrect IMEI numbers, account verification issues, or compatibility problems. If you can't resolve the issue yourself, our 24/7 support team is available to help. You can reach them via phone, email, or live chat, and they'll guide you through resolving any problems quickly and efficiently.

If your phone is locked to another carrier, you'll need to unlock it first before activating it on Verizon. Most carriers offer unlocking services, and the process varies depending on your previous carrier. You can contact your previous carrier to request an unlock code, or our support team can provide guidance on how to unlock your specific phone model. Once unlocked, you can follow our standard activation process. We recommend having your phone unlocked before starting the activation to ensure a smooth experience.
